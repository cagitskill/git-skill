# git-skill — features & things

A working notebook for my experience with AI Tools. A storyteller of products and features associated with GitHub. Period.

This repo is where I park the small stuff — things I figure out, things I try, and things I build while doing the job.

## What lives here

**Discoveries.** Short notes on things I run into and want to remember, drawn from public docs, my own testing, and general patterns — never from specific customer environments or conversations.

**Experiments.** Quick tests and proofs-of-concept. Some are rough, some are polished — both on purpose. They exist to answer a question, not to ship.

**Builds.** Small tools, scripts, and demos I've put together to make the role easier or to show something to a customer.

**Storytelling.** Occasional write-ups where I walk through a scenario as a way to learn the role better or share what I've picked up. Narrative over reference material.

## What doesn't live here

Personal projects. Ideas. Builds and Ships. This repo stays intentionally light.

Also off-limits: anything tied to a specific customer, internal-only material, unreleased features, or roadmap details.

## Infographics (GitHub Pages)

Visual, animated walk-throughs of technical concepts that come up in customer conversations. Live at **https://cagitskill.github.io/gitskill/** and built from the `/docs` folder.

Current set:
- **CI/CD Resiliency with Self-Hosted Runners** — what breaks, why it breaks, and how GitHub gives you the tools to fix it.
- **GitHub's MCP Server — How It Works** — how Copilot connects to your repos, issues, and PRs from the IDE, end to end.

## Why it's public

Sharing securely makes me more intentional about what I'm learning and sharing. Only sharing what can be shared publicly.
